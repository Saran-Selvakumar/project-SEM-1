I REMEMBER ABBU


Bangladesh, 1971: the war of independence from
Pakistan has torn through peaceful villages and
turned life upside down. In the midst of war, one
young girl holds on as she discovers the
world's unpredictability. During her father's prolonged
absence, she reminisces about the essence
of her abbu, an esteemed professor, loving community
leader, and now unexpected warrior.She is moved by
his quiet determination to preserve Bengali
language and culture in a struggle for autonomy. In his diaries,
her abbu describes the painful decisions he must
make because of the threat of war,
from embracing the brutality of taking up arms to the
struggle of moving his family from the
embattled city of Dhaka.
